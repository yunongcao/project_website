About WiFind

This project seeks to visualize the prevalence of Wi-Fi coverage throughout all five boroughs of New York City in order to understand accessibility levels citywide.Using an Android mobile application, users can measure and record the signal strength of Wi-Fi networks around them as they move through the city.

Our hope is that these readings can help inform a wide array of academic and social purposes, ranging from visualizing digital equality to finding the closest open Wi-Fi network.

The project team is from NYU CUSP, an university-wide research center focusing on urban systems and urban informatics. We aim to use data analytics to understand complex urban systems and ultimately improve quality of life for those living in cities throughout the world.

Getting App

Our Android mobile application measures and records the signal strength of Wi-Fi networks around users as users move through the city. Using our application will not interfere any other processes in your Android device.

The Map

The map shows the intensity of Wi-Fi signal at various locations in New York City based on the current data we have collected. To use this map, simply type in the name of network you want to show then choose an aggregation function and click the "SHOW DATA" button.

There are three aggregation functions available for choose. Choosing "Median" will show you the median value of signal intesity at each location. Likewise, choosing "Max" or "Average" will give you the maximum or average value of signal intesity at each location.
